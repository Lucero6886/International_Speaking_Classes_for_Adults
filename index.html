🧠 Learning New Skills as an Adult

A communicative English lesson for Adults (A2 - B2)

🎯 Learning Objectives:

  • Discuss the benefits and challenges of learning as an adult.
  • Use vocabulary related to learning, habits, and self-improvement.
  • Share personal experiences and strategies for mastering new skills.

🔥 1. Warm-Up

Let's get talking! Ask and answer these quick questions. Click the button if you need an example.

🎯 What is a new skill you want to learn this year?

"I really want to learn how to edit videos for my social media."

💻 Do you prefer learning online or in a classroom?

"I prefer learning online because I can study at my own pace."

📚 What was your favorite subject when you were in school?

"My favorite subject was History. I loved learning about the past."

🧗 Is it harder to learn new things now than when you were a child?

"Yes, I think so. Kids have more free time and absorb information faster."

⭐ Who is the most skilled person you know? What can they do?

"My father is very skilled. He knows how to fix any car engine."

📚 2. Vocabulary

Learn these words about learning and self-improvement. Click to see an example sentence.

1. To master (v)

To become highly skilled at doing something.

"It takes many years of practice to master playing the piano."
2. Challenging (adj)

Difficult in a way that tests your ability or determination.

"Learning English grammar can be very challenging at first."
3. To give up (v)

To stop trying to do something before you have finished.

"The puzzle was too hard, but he refused to give up."
4. Progress (n)

Movement forward towards a goal or improvement.

"I am making good progress with my driving lessons."
5. Overwhelming (adj)

Too great, difficult, or confusing to handle.

"Starting a new job can feel overwhelming on the first day."
6. To enroll (v)

To officially join a course, school, or program.

"I decided to enroll in a photography class this weekend."
7. Rewarding (adj)

Giving a feeling of happiness or satisfaction.

"Teaching others is a very rewarding experience."
8. To dedicate (v)

To give your time, effort, or energy to a specific task.

"You must dedicate at least 30 minutes a day to see results."

🗣️ 3. Main Speaking Questions

Take turns answering these questions. Use the buttons to get ideas or see sample answers if you get stuck!

Easy

1. What is the last new thing you learned how to do?

Ideas: A recipe, a software program for work, driving, using a new app.
Short: "The last thing I learned was how to bake banana bread."
Extended: "The last new thing I learned was how to use Excel for my job. I watched a few tutorials online and practiced entering data. Now, it's much easier for me to organize my files."
Easy

2. Do you like watching tutorial videos on YouTube? Why or why not?

Ideas: Visual learning, free information, sometimes too long, easy to pause and rewind.
Short: "Yes, I love them because they are free and easy to follow."
Extended: "Yes, I think YouTube is the best teacher! I am a visual learner, so watching someone actually do the task helps me understand it better. Plus, I can pause and rewind if I get confused."
Easy

3. Do you prefer learning alone or with other people?

Ideas: Quiet focus, no distractions vs. motivation, group energy, asking a partner for help.
Short: "I prefer learning alone because I can focus better."
Extended: "It depends on the skill. If I am reading or studying grammar, I prefer to be alone in a quiet room. But if I am learning a sport or a language, I like learning with others because it is more fun and interactive."
Medium

4. Why do many adults stop learning new skills after they finish university?

Ideas: Busy with jobs/family, too tired, lack of motivation, no tests to force them.
Short: "Because they are too busy with their careers and families."
Extended: "I think many adults stop learning because life gets overwhelming. They have to work full-time, take care of children, and manage a home. By the end of the day, they are simply too tired to open a book or enroll in a class."
Medium

5. How do you find the time to learn something new in your busy schedule?

Ideas: Waking up early, weekend mornings, learning during commutes, dedicating 15 mins a day.
Short: "I try to practice for 15 minutes before I go to bed."
Extended: "It is very challenging, but I try to use my commute time. While I am riding the bus, I listen to podcasts or use language apps. I also try to dedicate 20 minutes every Sunday morning to review what I learned."
Medium

6. What is the most challenging part of learning a new language as an adult?

Ideas: Pronunciation, memorizing vocabulary, fear of making mistakes, grammar rules.
Short: "The hardest part is remembering new vocabulary words."
Extended: "For me, the most challenging part is speaking without fear. As adults, we are afraid of sounding silly or making mistakes. Children don't care if they make a mistake, which is why they learn to speak so much faster."
Medium

7. Describe a time when you wanted to give up on learning something, but you kept going.

Ideas: Driving a car, a difficult software, a sport (swimming/biking), playing an instrument.
Short: "I wanted to give up learning to drive because it was scary."
Extended: "When I first started learning to play the guitar, my fingers hurt a lot and I couldn't make it sound good. I wanted to give up after a month. But my friend encouraged me to keep practicing, and eventually, I made progress and played my first song."
Opinion

8. Do you think everyone should learn basic coding or computer programming?

Ideas: Essential for the future, helps logical thinking vs. too specialized, not needed for every job.
Short: "No, I think only people who work in tech need to learn it."
Extended: "I think having basic computer skills is necessary, but actual coding is not for everyone. While it teaches logical thinking, it is a very specialized skill. People should focus on skills relevant to their own careers and passions."
Opinion

9. "It is impossible to teach an old dog new tricks." Do you agree with this idiom?

Ideas: Brain plasticity, lifelong learning, older people have more patience, habit breaking is hard.
Short: "I disagree. Anyone can learn at any age if they try hard."
Extended: "I completely disagree. While it might take longer for older people to process certain new information, they have more patience and life experience to help them understand concepts. Lifelong learning is definitely possible if you are dedicated."
Opinion

10. Should companies give their employees paid time off to learn new personal skills?

Ideas: Happier employees, better mental health, companies lose money, skill should relate to the job.
Short: "Yes, because it makes employees happier and more productive."
Extended: "I believe companies should offer some time for learning, even if it's just a few hours a month. When employees learn new things, they feel more motivated and creative. Ultimately, a happy and developing employee will perform better at work."

🎭 4. Role-Play Scenarios

Choose a partner and act out the following real-life situations.

Scenario A: The Hesitant Learner

Student A: You want to join an evening Salsa dance class and you are trying to convince your friend to join you.

Student B: You are very hesitant. You think you are "too old" to learn dancing and you are worried you will look silly.

  • "Come on, it will be so much fun!"
  • "I have two left feet. I'm going to embarrass myself."
  • "Everyone there is a beginner too."
  • "Well, maybe I can just watch the first class..."

Scenario B: The Time Management Coach

Student A: You really want to learn how to play the guitar, but you keep telling yourself you have "absolutely no time."

Student B: You are a time management coach. You must give Student A practical advice on how to dedicate time to this new skill.

  • "By the time I get home from work, I'm exhausted."
  • "Can you wake up just 15 minutes earlier?"
  • "I spend an hour scrolling on my phone in bed."
  • "Replace that phone time with guitar practice."
