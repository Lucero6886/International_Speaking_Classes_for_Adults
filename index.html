🤝 Professional Problem Solving

Mastering effective communication, conflict resolution, and teamwork in the modern workplace.

💼 Working Adults 📊 Mixed B1-B2 ⏱️ 60 Minutes 💻 Online Class

🎯 Session Objectives (Today, we will...):

  1. Describe past workplace challenges and how they were resolved.
  2. Propose logical solutions to common professional problems using constructive language.
  3. Role-play a business negotiation to reach a compromise politely.

☕ 1. Warm-up: Career Check-In

🎤 FACILITATOR TIP Ask participants to type a quick word in the chat, then invite 2-3 professionals to share their experiences on mic.

1. What is the most challenging part of your current job? 🏢
B1 Core The hardest part is dealing with angry customers. It can be very stressful.
B2 Extended For me, the main challenge is cross-departmental collaboration. Ensuring everyone is aligned on the same goals requires a lot of communication.
2. How do you usually handle a missed deadline? ⏰
B1 Core I usually tell my manager immediately and try to finish the work as fast as I can.
B2 Extended I proactively communicate the delay to stakeholders, explain the reason, and propose a realistic new timeline to manage their expectations.
3. Do you prefer working independently or in a team? Why? 🤝
B1 Core I prefer working in a team because we can share ideas and help each other.
B2 Extended It depends on the task. For brainstorming and complex problem-solving, I thrive in a team environment. However, for deep focus work like data analysis, I prefer working independently.
4. Have you ever experienced a miscommunication at work? What happened? 🗣️
B1 Core Yes. I sent an email to a colleague, but they misunderstood my tone and got upset. We had to call each other to fix it.
B2 Extended Absolutely. Last year, vague instructions led our team to work on the wrong project requirements for a week. Since then, I always clarify expectations in writing after a meeting.

📚 2. Business Vocabulary

🗣️ FACILITATOR TIP Read the word clearly. Ask participants to read the example sentences to practice pronunciation in context.

Miscommunication 📵
(mis-kuh-myoo-ni-KAY-shun) - Noun

Meaning: Failure to communicate clearly, leading to a mistake or misunderstanding.

B1
"A simple miscommunication caused a one-week delay in our project."
Collaborate 🧩
(kuh-LAB-uh-rate) - Verb

Meaning: To work together with someone to achieve a goal or produce something.

B1
"Our marketing team needs to collaborate closely with the sales department."
Compromise ⚖️
(KOM-pruh-mize) - Noun/Verb

Meaning: An agreement reached where both sides give up something they wanted.

B2
"We couldn't agree on the budget, so we had to find a compromise."
Clarify 🔎
(KLAR-ih-fy) - Verb

Meaning: To make something easier to understand by explaining it in more detail.

B1
"Could you please clarify what you meant in the second paragraph of your email?"
Propose 💡
(pruh-POZE) - Verb

Meaning: To suggest a plan, idea, or solution for people to consider.

B2
"I would like to propose a new strategy to increase our quarterly sales."
Escalate 📈
(ES-kuh-late) - Verb

Meaning: To involve someone more senior (like a manager) to help resolve a difficult issue.

B2
"If the client remains unhappy, we will need to escalate the issue to the director."
Constructive feedback 📝
(kun-STRUK-tiv FEED-bak) - Noun Phrase

Meaning: Useful comments or suggestions intended to help someone improve.

B2
"A good manager provides constructive feedback rather than just criticizing."
Take initiative 🚀
(take ih-NISH-uh-tiv) - Verb Phrase

Meaning: To assess and start things independently without needing to be told.

B2
"Employers love employees who take initiative and solve problems proactively."

🧩 3. Corporate Speaking Frameworks

Use these professional structures to communicate effectively in meetings.

Level 1: Identifying Issues (B1)

"The main problem seems to be [Noun/V-ing], which means that [Negative Result]."

Example: "The main problem seems to be a lack of communication, which means that we are missing our deadlines."

Your turn! Use these ideas:
low budget unclear instructions we cannot hire more staff the client is confused

Level 2: Proposing Solutions (B2)

"I highly recommend that we [Action], otherwise [Negative Consequence]."

Example: "I highly recommend that we clarify the project scope today, otherwise the client will lose trust in us."

Your turn! Use these ideas:
schedule a meeting extend the deadline the project will fail employees will burn out

🎤 4. Case Study Discussion

💬 FACILITATOR TIP Ask participants to choose one question to answer. Encourage them to draw on real workplace experiences.

1. Describe a time you successfully collaborated with a difficult coworker. How did you do it? 🤝
B1 Core listened to them set clear rules
B1: I had a coworker who always interrupted me. I decided to schedule a 1-on-1 meeting. I listened to his ideas first, and then he was more willing to listen to mine.
B2: I once worked with a colleague who was very resistant to change. Instead of arguing, I took the initiative to ask for their feedback on my proposal. By making them feel valued, we were able to compromise and collaborate smoothly.
2. How do you handle receiving negative or constructive feedback from a manager? 📝
B2 Extended don't take it personally ask for examples improve performance
B1: It is hard at first, but I try to stay calm. I ask my manager to clarify what I did wrong so I can fix it next time.
B2: I view constructive feedback as a tool for professional growth. While it can be uncomfortable, I try not to take it personally. I usually ask for specific examples so I can take initiative and improve my performance immediately.
3. "The customer is always right." Do you agree with this statement in modern business? Why or why not? 💼
B2 Challenge set boundaries protect employees find a compromise
B1: I don't agree completely. Sometimes customers are rude or ask for impossible things. We should respect them, but we must protect our staff too.
B2: I disagree. While exceptional customer service is crucial, blindly believing the customer is always right can lead to toxic situations for employees. We should listen to their concerns and try to find a compromise, but we must also set professional boundaries when a customer is unreasonable.

🎭 6. Negotiation Role-Play

👥 FACILITATOR TIP Divide into pairs in breakout rooms for 10 minutes. Encourage professional tone and polite phrasing.

🛑 Situation: The Unrealistic Deadline

Context: A Project Manager has just assigned a massive presentation to a Team Member, due tomorrow morning. It is impossible to finish without working all night.

👔 Role A (Project Manager)

  • Explain that the CEO suddenly requested this presentation.
  • Acknowledge it's short notice, but insist it's high priority.
  • Ask the Team Member what they need to get it done tonight.

💼 Role B (Team Member)

  • Express concern about the quality of work if rushed.
  • Politely set a boundary (you cannot work all night).
  • Propose a compromise (e.g., deliver a summary tomorrow, full report by Friday).

Manager: "I know it's a tight turnaround, but the CEO needs this by 9 AM tomorrow. Can you make it happen?"

Team Member: "I understand it's urgent. However, doing a full 50-page analysis tonight will compromise the quality and accuracy of the data."

Manager: "What if I get someone from IT to help you gather the data?"

Team Member: "That helps, but I'd like to propose a compromise. I can deliver a 5-page executive summary for the 9 AM meeting, and provide the detailed report by Thursday. Does that work?"

⚡ 7. Business Discourse Markers (Click to flip!)

Use these phrases to sound more diplomatic and professional in meetings.

Let's look at the bigger picture...

(Click to flip)

Function: Refocusing the team on the main goal rather than small details.

Example: "Instead of arguing about the font color, let's look at the bigger picture: what does the client want?"

I see where you're coming from, but...

(Click to flip)

Function: Disagreeing politely by validating their opinion first.

Example: "I see where you're coming from regarding the budget, but we really need this software."

Moving forward...

(Click to flip)

Function: Shifting the focus from past mistakes to future actions.

Example: "We missed the deadline this time. Moving forward, let's have weekly check-in meetings."

To get back on track...

(Click to flip)

Function: Stopping off-topic discussion and returning to the agenda.

Example: "That's an interesting point about the office party, but to get back on track, how is the marketing report?"

🧑‍🏫 Facilitator Notes

Managing Professional Learners (Mixed B1-B2):

  • Lack of Ideas: If professionals are hesitant to share real workplace issues due to confidentiality, suggest they use "a hypothetical situation" or "a situation a friend experienced."
  • Direct Translation Tone: B1 learners often translate directly, which can sound rude in English (e.g., "You are wrong"). Teach "softeners" and diplomatic language (e.g., "I see your point, but...").
  • Grammar vs. Communication: Prioritize successful negotiation and problem-solving over perfect grammar. Praise the *strategy* they used to resolve the conflict.

Polite Correction Strategies:

  • "Your point was very clear. To sound a bit more diplomatic in an email, we could say..."
  • "Great solution! Let's try using our new word 'propose' to frame that idea."
