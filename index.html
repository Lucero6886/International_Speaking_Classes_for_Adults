Lesson Objectives

  • Discuss practical strategies and challenges in learning English.
  • Use vocabulary related to language acquisition and communication.
  • Express opinions and share personal professional experiences fluently.

1. Warm-Up Discussion ☕

Let's get started. Read the questions and share your thoughts to warm up your speaking skills.

1. Why did you decide to start learning English? 🎯
"I started learning English because it is essential for my career growth and helps me communicate with international clients."
2. Where do you use English the most in your daily life? 📱
"I mostly use English at work to write emails, and at home when I watch movies or read news articles online."
3. Do you feel nervous when speaking to foreigners? Why? 😅
"Yes, sometimes I feel nervous because I worry about making grammar mistakes or that they won't understand my accent."
4. What is your favorite way to practice listening? 🎧
"My favorite way to practice listening is by listening to podcasts while commuting to work."
5. Have you ever traveled to an English-speaking country? ✈️
"Yes, I visited London a few years ago. It was challenging at first, but a great experience to practice speaking."

2. Essential Vocabulary 📖

Familiarize yourself with these terms to enhance your communication today.

Fluency
The ability to speak or write a language smoothly and easily.
"Her fluency in English helped her secure the international sales manager position."
Misunderstand
To fail to understand someone correctly.
"I'm sorry, I think I misunderstood your instructions. Could you repeat them?"
Immerse
To surround yourself completely with something, like a culture or language.
"The best way to learn is to immerse yourself in the language by living abroad."
Essential
Absolutely necessary or extremely important.
"Daily practice is essential if you want to improve your pronunciation."
Confident
Feeling sure of your own abilities or qualities.
"She felt much more confident giving her presentation after practicing it three times."
Context
The situation or information surrounding a word that helps explain its meaning.
"If you don't know a word, try to guess its meaning from the context of the sentence."
Overcome
To succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty.
"It takes time to overcome the fear of speaking a foreign language in public."
Accent
A distinctive way of pronouncing a language associated with a particular region.
"Having an accent is perfectly fine as long as people can understand you clearly."

3. Speaking Practice 🗣️

Develop your fluency. Choose your challenge level and use the Idea Bank for inspiration.

Easy
1. How often do you actively practice speaking English?
  • Every day with international colleagues.
  • A few times a week during my English classes.
  • Rarely, mostly I just read and listen.
"I actively practice speaking about two or three times a week during my online classes."
"I try to practice speaking every single day. Even if I don't have a conversation partner, I often talk to myself in English while doing household chores or driving. It really helps me feel more comfortable with the language."
Easy
2. Do you prefer watching movies with or without subtitles? Why?
  • With English subtitles to connect words and sounds.
  • Without subtitles to challenge my listening skills.
  • With subtitles in my native language so I can relax.
"I prefer using English subtitles so I can see how new words are spelled."
"It depends on my mood. If I am studying, I watch with English subtitles so I can learn new vocabulary and connect pronunciation with spelling. But if I just want to relax, I watch without subtitles to challenge my listening comprehension naturally."
Easy
3. What is the hardest part of learning English for you?
  • Understanding fast native speakers.
  • Remembering phrasal verbs and idioms.
  • Overcoming the fear of making grammar mistakes.
"The hardest part for me is understanding native speakers when they talk very fast."
"For me, the most challenging aspect is remembering phrasal verbs. There are so many of them, and their meanings often don't make logical sense based on the individual words. I have to memorize them entirely from context."
Medium
4. How can making mistakes actually help you learn a language?
  • Mistakes show you what you need to focus on.
  • When someone corrects you, you remember it better.
  • It helps you overcome perfectionism and just communicate.
"Mistakes are helpful because they highlight the specific areas where you need to improve."
"Making mistakes is a crucial part of the learning process. When I make a mistake and someone corrects me, the embarrassment actually helps me remember the correct rule for the future. It also teaches you that communication is more important than perfection."
Medium
5. Describe a time when you successfully communicated in English in a real-life situation.
  • Giving directions to a lost tourist in my city.
  • Handling a business call with a client from abroad.
  • Ordering food smoothly at a restaurant during a vacation.
"I felt very proud when I successfully ordered a complicated meal at a restaurant in New York."
"A few months ago, a tourist stopped me on the street to ask for directions to the train station. I was nervous at first, but I managed to explain the route clearly using simple vocabulary. When they thanked me and found their way, it gave me a huge boost of confidence."
Medium
6. What are some effective ways to immerse yourself in English without traveling?
  • Change the language on your phone and computer to English.
  • Listen to English podcasts and news while commuting.
  • Join international online communities or gaming groups.
"You can change your phone's language setting to English and listen to English podcasts daily."
"There are many ways to create immersion at home. Personally, I changed all my devices to English and I only consume news from international websites. I also joined a few Discord servers related to my hobbies where everyone communicates in English, which forces me to read and write daily."
Medium
7. How does knowing English specifically help your career or future goals?
  • It opens up opportunities to work for multinational companies.
  • It allows me to read professional research and literature in my field.
  • It is required for communicating with overseas suppliers.
"Knowing English is essential for me because I want to work for a multinational tech company."
"In my industry, almost all the latest research, software documentation, and training materials are published in English first. Being fluent allows me to stay updated with industry trends and opens up opportunities for promotions that require international travel."
Opinion
8. In your opinion, is perfect grammar or a wide vocabulary more important for communication?
  • Vocabulary: Without grammar, you can convey little; without vocabulary, nothing.
  • Grammar: It prevents serious misunderstandings in professional settings.
  • Both: You need a balance of both to sound professional.
"In my opinion, vocabulary is more important because you can still express ideas even with bad grammar."
"From my perspective, a wide vocabulary is far more important for daily communication. If you know the right words, a native speaker can usually guess your meaning from the context, even if your verb tenses are wrong. However, if you don't have the vocabulary, conversation stops completely."
Opinion
9. Do you think you need a native-like accent to be a successful English speaker?
  • No: As long as you are clear and articulate, an accent shows your unique background.
  • Yes: In some strict corporate environments, a strong accent might cause bias.
  • It depends: Clarity is more important than sounding exactly like an American or Brit.
"I completely disagree; clear pronunciation is important, but a native accent is not necessary."
"I strongly believe that a native-like accent is not necessary. English is a global language spoken by billions of non-native speakers. As long as your pronunciation is clear enough to avoid misunderstandings, your accent is just a beautiful reflection of your cultural identity."
Opinion
10. Some people say adults cannot learn a language as well as children. Do you agree?
  • Agree: Children absorb languages naturally without overthinking rules.
  • Disagree: Adults have better study habits, discipline, and understanding of grammar.
  • Partial: Adults might struggle with pronunciation, but learn vocabulary faster.
"I disagree. Adults may struggle with accents, but they are better at understanding complex grammar."
"I only partially agree with this statement. It is true that children acquire pronunciation and intonation more naturally. However, adults have cognitive advantages; we understand abstract concepts, have disciplined study routines, and can actively associate new vocabulary with existing knowledge. We just need to overcome the fear of making mistakes."

4. Real-Life Roleplay 🤝

Practice these common scenarios with a partner. Apply the vocabulary you learned.

Scenario 1: The Networking Event

Situation: You are attending an international conference. You sit next to a colleague from another country. Introduce yourself, explain what you do, and make small talk.

Roles: You (Professional) & Foreign Colleague.

You: "Hello, mind if I sit here? I'm [Name], I work in [Department]."

Colleague: "Nice to meet you. I'm Alex. How are you enjoying the conference?"

You: "It's been great. The last speaker was very insightful. Where are you visiting from?"

Colleague: "I flew in from Canada. Is this your first time at this event?"

Scenario 2: The Misunderstanding

Situation: You are on a phone call with an airline customer service agent. They are speaking very quickly, and you didn't catch the details of your flight change.

Roles: You (Customer) & Customer Service Agent.

Agent: "So your flight is rebooked for the 14th departing at 16:00 from gate B22, is that confirmed?"

You: "I'm sorry, I think I misunderstood that. Could you please speak a little slower?"

Agent: "Certainly. Your new flight is on the 14th..."

You: "Thank you. Let me repeat that back to ensure I have the essential details correct."
